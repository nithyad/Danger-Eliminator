Emergency

Unconsciousness

What is unconsciousness?

Unconsciousness is when a person suddenly becomes unresponsive. Unconsciousness occurs when a person is suddenly unable to respond to stimuli and appears to be asleep. A person may be unconscious for a few seconds or for longer periods of time.

Causes of long-term unconsciousness

  • A car accident


  • Severe blood loss


  • A blow to the chest or head


  • Drug overdose


  • Alcohol poisoning


Causes of temporary unconsciousness

<
  • Low blood sugar


  • Syncope: loss of consciousness when the brain is deprived of blood flow


  • Dehydration


  • Problems with heart rhythm


  • Straining


  • Hyperventilating


What to Do? If a person is unconscious or has a mental status, follow these steps:

  1. Call 911.


  2. Check airway, breathing, and pulse constantly.


  3. If necessary, began CPR.


  4. If you don't think that he or she has a spinal injury, roll the person toward you onto his or her side. Bend top leg so that both the hip and knee form right angles.


  5. If you believe there is a spinal injury, do not move the person!


  6. If they vomit, roll their body to their side and keep their head and body in the same position as you roll


  7. Keep them warm until help arrives


DO NOT ...

  • DO NOT give an unconscious person any food or drink


  • DO NOT leave the person alone


  • DO NOT place a pillow under a unconscious person


  • DO NOT slap an unconscious persons face or splash water on there face to revive them
