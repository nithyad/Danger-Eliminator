Emergency

Choking

Signs if someone is choking:

  1. A child who is choking may wave their arms or grab at their throat.


  2. Older children and adults may hold their neck one or both hands, also known as the universal sign of choking.


  3. A person is struggling to breathe and is taking gasps to gain air.


  4. A person is coughing and gagging.


  5. Bluish lips or skin


What to do: the Heimlich Maneuver

For a sitting or standing person:

  1. Position yourself behind the person.


  2. Reach your arms around the choking person's waist. You may have to kneel for children.


  3. Place your fist with the thumb side towards just above the person's belly button.


  4. Grasp your first tightly with your other hand.


  5. Make quick, inward thrusts with your fist until the person spits out the object.


If the person is lying on his back:

  1. Straddle the person facing the head.


  2. Push your grasped fist upward and inward in a movement similar to the standing up heimlich maneuver.
